Fried Plantain (Dodo)

Description

Dodo, also known as Fried Plantains, is a popular Nigerian dish made from ripe plantains that are sliced and deep-fried to a golden crisp. The sweet and savory flavor of the plantains makes them a perfect side dish or snack. Dodo is commonly served alongside rice dishes like Jollof Rice or as a side to grilled meats and fish. The dish is a favorite at parties, street food stalls, and in many Nigerian households.

Ingredients

  • 2 ripe plantains (yellow and slightly soft)
  • Vegetable oil for frying
  • Salt (to taste)
  • Optional: chili powder or paprika for a spicier version

Steps

  1. Peel the plantains and slice them into diagonal or round pieces, about 1/2 inch thick.
  2. Heat the vegetable oil in a frying pan over medium heat. Ensure thereâ€™s enough oil to submerge the plantain slices.
  3. Once the oil is hot, gently add the plantain slices to the pan, being careful not to overcrowd the pan.
  4. Fry the plantains for about 3-4 minutes on each side, or until golden brown and crispy.
  5. Remove the fried plantains from the oil and place them on a paper towel-lined plate to absorb excess oil.
  6. Sprinkle with salt while still hot. If desired, you can add a pinch of chili powder or paprika for a spicier kick.
  7. Serve the Dodo hot as a side dish or snack, and enjoy with Jollof rice, grilled meats, or a dipping sauce!
