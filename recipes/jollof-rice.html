Jollof Rice

Description

Jollof Rice is a classic West African dish, particularly popular in Nigeria. It is a one-pot dish made with rice, tomatoes, onions, and a mix of spices that give it a rich and vibrant flavor. Jollof rice is often served at parties, celebrations, and everyday meals. The combination of the stewed tomato base and seasonings gives it a savory, slightly spicy taste that pairs well with grilled meats or fried plantains.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups long-grain parboiled rice
  • 1/4 cup vegetable oil
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 1 red bell pepper, chopped
  • 1 green bell pepper, chopped
  • 4 medium tomatoes, blended
  • 2 tablespoons tomato paste
  • 2 teaspoons curry powder
  • 2 teaspoons thyme
  • 1 teaspoon white pepper
  • 1 teaspoon ginger, grated
  • 1 teaspoon garlic, minced
  • 4 cups chicken or vegetable broth
  • Salt to taste
  • 1 cup frozen peas (optional)
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh parsley (optional)

Steps

  1. Rinse the rice in cold water and set it aside to drain.
  2. Heat the vegetable oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add the chopped onions and sauté until they become soft and translucent.
  3. Add the chopped bell peppers, ginger, and garlic, and cook for another 3-4 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  4. Stir in the blended tomatoes, tomato paste, curry powder, thyme, white pepper, and a pinch of salt. Let this cook on medium heat for about 10-15 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the tomato mixture thickens and reduces.
  5. Pour in the chicken or vegetable broth and bring to a boil. Once boiling, add the rice, stir well, and reduce the heat to low.
  6. Cover the pot and let the rice simmer for about 20-25 minutes, or until the rice is fully cooked and the liquid is absorbed. Stir occasionally to ensure the rice doesn’t burn at the bottom of the pot.
  7. If using, add the frozen peas during the last 5 minutes of cooking to allow them to heat through.
  8. Once the rice is done, fluff it with a fork and garnish with chopped parsley, if desired. Serve hot with grilled chicken, fried plantains, or any side dish of your choice.
