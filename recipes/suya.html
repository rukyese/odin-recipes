Suya

Description

Suya is a popular Nigerian street food made from skewered and grilled beef or chicken, marinated in a flavorful spice mix. The seasoning for Suya includes a special suya pepper mix (called Yaji), which gives the dish its signature spiciness and earthy flavor. Often served with sliced onions, tomatoes, and spicy pepper sauce, Suya is enjoyed as a quick snack or a savory main dish at parties, gatherings, and outdoor events.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb beef or chicken (boneless, cut into cubes or strips)
  • 1/4 cup groundnut (peanut) powder
  • 2 tablespoons ground cayenne pepper (adjust to taste)
  • 1 tablespoon paprika
  • 1 tablespoon ground ginger
  • 1 tablespoon ground garlic
  • 1 tablespoon onion powder
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon ground thyme
  • 1 teaspoon ground white pepper
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • 1 tablespoon bouillon powder (optional)
  • Salt to taste
  • Skewers (wooden or metal)
  • Onions and tomatoes (for garnish, optional)

Steps

  1. In a mixing bowl, combine the groundnut powder, cayenne pepper, paprika, ground ginger, garlic, onion powder, cumin, thyme, white pepper, bouillon powder, and salt. Stir until the spices are evenly mixed.
  2. Add the vegetable oil to the spice mix and stir until it forms a paste.
  3. Coat the beef or chicken pieces with the spice paste, making sure each piece is well covered. For best results, let the meat marinate for at least 1-2 hours, or overnight in the refrigerator.
  4. Thread the marinated meat onto the skewers, spacing them evenly.
  5. Preheat the grill to medium-high heat. Grill the skewers for about 10-15 minutes, turning occasionally, until the meat is cooked to your desired level of doneness and has a slight char on the outside.
  6. Once cooked, remove the skewers from the grill and let them rest for a couple of minutes.
  7. Serve the Suya with sliced onions, tomatoes, and extra spicy pepper sauce on the side, if desired. Enjoy!
